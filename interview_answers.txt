Interview Questions and Answers

1. What is phishing?
Phishing is when attackers try to trick people into giving away sensitive information (like passwords or credit card numbers) by pretending to be a trustworthy source.

2. How to identify a phishing email?
Look for suspicious sender addresses, urgent or threatening language, mismatched links, requests for personal information, and spelling or grammar mistakes.

3. What is email spoofing?
Email spoofing is when someone fakes the sender address to make an email look like it comes from a trusted source.

4. Why are phishing emails dangerous?
They can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and unauthorized access to personal or company data.

7. What actions should be taken on suspected phishing emails?
Do not click links or download attachments. Report the email to your IT department or email provider, and delete it.

8. How do attackers use social engineering in phishing?
They manipulate people into trusting them, often by creating a sense of urgency or impersonating authority figures.
