    <h1>Phishing Email Analysis Report</h1>
    <h2>Sample Email</h2>
    <pre>
From: support@paypaI.com
Subject: Urgent: Your Account Has Been Compromised!
To: user@example.com

Dear Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your PayPal account. To secure your account, please log in immediately using the link below and update your password.

Click here: http://paypaI.com/login

If you do not act within 24 hours, your account will be suspended.

Thank you,
PayPal Security Team
    </pre>

    <h2>Phishing Indicators Found</h2>
    <table>
        <tr><th>Indicator</th><th>Example/Checklist</th><th>Found?</th></tr>
        <tr><td>Spoofed sender address</td><td>“support@paypaI.com” (note the capital “I” instead of “l”)</td><td>Yes</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Urgent/threatening tone</td><td>“Urgent: Your Account Has Been Compromised!”, “act within 24 hours”</td><td>Yes</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Suspicious link</td><td>“http://paypaI.com/login” (not the real PayPal domain)</td><td>Yes</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Request for credentials</td><td>Asks to “log in” and “update your password”</td><td>Yes</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Spelling/grammar errors</td><td>None obvious, but domain spoofing is present</td><td>No</td></tr>
    </table>

    <h2>Conclusion</h2>
    <p>
        This email contains multiple phishing indicators, including a spoofed sender address, urgent language, and a suspicious link that does not match the legitimate PayPal domain. Users should avoid clicking on such links and never enter credentials on unknown sites.
    </p>
